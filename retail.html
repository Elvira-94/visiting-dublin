Retail Attractions

Where to Stay?

The Grafton Hotel

Nestled in the heart of the city centre at Dublin 2, our hotel is only a stone's throw from some of Dublin's most renowned bars and restaurants. Before embarking on some high-street shopping or local attractions, why not start your authentic Irish experience at our very own "Bartley's Bar & Restaurant", a leading institution since the 1960s and sheltering some of the greats like Elizabeth Taylor and Richard Burton.

With pre-arrival contactless check-in to one of our 127 luxurious hotel rooms, you are free to explore everything Dublin has to offer at your own pace before returning for what is guaranteed to be a great night's sleep. Book a room here.

Where to Dine?

The Port house

The Port House on South William Street, Dublin 2 is a cosy, romantic restaurant set within candle lit cave setting, offering the very best in traditional Spanish tapas & Basque pintxos, along with a comprehensive list of ports, wines and sherries. This cosy, relaxing eatery is the perfect venue for small private group parties such as graduations, family gatherings and corporate dinners or meetings.

Opened first in 2006 we realised we had something special in our little cave on South William Street in Dublin 2! Twelve months later, we opened another little cave in Temple Bar and our third in a cottage in Dundrum Town's Centre's Pembrook District. Book a table here.

Where to shop?

Grafton Street

Grafton Street, Dublin is one of the Europe's busiest shopping streets. It is also dubbed as "Ireland's Premier Trading Street" Main shopping street in Dublin, lined with boutiques containing the creations of Ireland's trendy designers.

It runs from St Stephen's Green in the south (at the highest point of the street) to College Green in the north (the lowest point).
