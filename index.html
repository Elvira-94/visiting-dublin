Dublin City

Gateway to Ireland

Dublin is Ireland's largest city and is seen as the gateway to The Emerald Isle. If you go to Ireland, you will almost certainly land in Dublin and begin your journey there.

Dublin on the other hand, is far from a stopover on your Irish vacation: this dynamic city has lots of attractions to suit all visitors and easily warrants a few days in itself.

Is it easy to get around Dublin?

Driving in Dublin is not usually recommend because the roads are so busy. In the capital, public transport is usually the best way to get around.

When it comes to getting around the city, the three most popular methods are: Dublin bus services, NOW Dublin bikes and Taxi Cabs.

Where to stay in Dublin?

Dublin has a wealth of accomodation options to suit all budgets. When choosing a hotel in Dublin, it all comes down to price and budget. The most expensive clusters are found near tourist areas of Temple Bar (city centre) and St. Stephen's Green (a little further south).

Then there's a good number of B&B's in Dublin city like Aishling House & Gleorga Guest house.

Our FAQ's

When is the best time to visit Dublin?

Much of this decision depends on your vacation budeget, what you want to see and do, and when you can get away. Visit during the low season, which runs from November through early March. The days are becoming shorter and certain attractions might be closed. Visit during the peak season, which runs from late May to August. This is an ideal time to visit Dublin.

What's the weather like in Dublin?

Because the weather is so volatile, it usually only rains for a short amount of time. This implies that if you get wet, it will only be a small amount. Bring proper attire - you're in Ireland, not California - a hat, umbrella, or hooded coat should serve.

However, are Irish summers hot? We wish, but they're the ideal temperature for exploring a city: 17-23°C (63-73°F). Sometimes you might even get a cool day 13-17°C (55-63°F), most commonly in August. So it's not "T-shirt and shorts" weather, except for the locals. Trousers, shirt and a light jacket would be best.

What is tipping etiquette like in Dublin?

In Ireland, tipping is normally expected in the service industry, including restaurants and tour guiding.

For restaurants, tips may be in the form of a 10-15% service charge which would be reflected on your reciept. Four Tour guides, the tip is normally 15-30%.

Do I need a visa?

Please contact your local Irish embassy or other diplomatic official before travelling to find out if you need a visa to enter Ireland by air, sea, or land. An Irish visa is a certificate that is place on your passport or travel document to indicate that you ar authorised to arrive in the country subject to any other conditions of landing being fulfilled.
